5 Essential Tax Compliance Tips for Small Businesses in Zimbabwe

Kudakwashe Ndaza

May 15, 2025

### Share:

6 min read

Navigating tax compliance can be challenging for small businesses in Zimbabwe, especially with the evolving fiscalisation requirements. This guide provides five essential tips to help you stay compliant and avoid costly penalties while focusing on growing your business.

1. Understand Your Tax Obligations

### As a business owner, it's crucial to understand the various taxes that may apply to your business:

### Income Tax:

Tax on your business profits

### Value Added Tax (VAT):

Currently at 14.5% for registered businesses

### PAYE (Pay As You Earn):

For employees' salaries

### Withholding Taxes:

On certain payments to suppliers

### Customs Duty:

For imported goods

Asset purchases and depreciation schedules

Consider using accounting software to automate record-keeping and reduce the risk of errors.

2. Understand Your Tax Obligations

Different businesses have different tax obligations depending on their legal structure, industry, and revenue. Be aware of:

Income tax requirements and deadlines

VAT registration and filing requirements

PAYE obligations if you have employees

Any industry-specific taxes or levies

3. Stay Updated on Tax Law Changes

### Tax laws and regulations can change frequently. Stay informed about:

Annual budget announcements

ZIMRA circulars and updates

Changes in tax rates or thresholds

New compliance requirements

Consider subscribing to tax updates from ZIMRA or consulting with a tax professional to stay current.

4. Implement a Fiscalisation Solution

### ZIMRA requires certain businesses to use fiscalised devices for recording sales. Ensure you:

Use ZIMRA-approved fiscal devices

Regularly update your fiscal devices

Keep backup records of all fiscalised transactions

Train staff on proper use of fiscal devices

5. Plan for Tax Payments

### Avoid cash flow issues by planning for your tax obligations throughout the year:

Set aside funds regularly for tax payments

Make quarterly estimated tax payments if required

Take advantage of tax planning opportunities

Consider working with an accountant to optimize your tax position

By implementing these tips, you can reduce stress at tax time and focus on growing your business with confidence.
